This semester was when I felt like I'm going to have to divert my focus again on a job. I gave myself that last 4 months to focus on studying, so I wanted to dedicate more time and be more deliberate towards securing a job. And so, I only took 1 class. At the time of writing this post, this is the hardest class as rated on the [MSDS Hub](https://msdshub.com/).

![ADL Rating](/assets/images/adl-rating.png)

I'll attribute this rating to the fact that this is a new class and the Central Limit Theorem hasn't kicked into action just yet.

This course was so theoretically dense though. I can see why people gave it such a difficult rating.

The assignments were very reasonable. The came in the form of "complete x out of y theoretical questions + choose your coding question" and I think assignments like these are the best form of assignments because they give you the freedom to pick which torture device you want to be destroyed with.

![Pick Your Poison](/assets/images/memes/torture.jpeg)

I'm joking ... its actually a good assignment because you have the freedom to choose your questions. Some questions are objectively harder than others, but just having the ability to pick something lets you appreciate how bad it COULD'VE been. It also makes the assignment smaller than it actually is. A lot of "doing the assignment" is mental, so just helping get over the mental block is much appreciated.

The coding assignments were also really good. We'd start off with some starter code and then implement the "generative" part of it and the output was almost always really cool.

The content of the course itself was very dense though. I whole field of Generative AI is new to me and very mathematically complex. Manipulating probability distributions is not easy and, as I've come to find out, sometimes doesn't have closed form solutions.

I did happen to watch a lot of YouTube videos about these concepts in my free time, so maybe that helped internalize the concepts I was studying a more easily. Some channels I'd recommend:

1. [Deepia](https://www.youtube.com/@Deepia-ls2fo)
2. [3Blue1Brown](https://www.youtube.com/channel/UCYO_jab_esuFRV4b17AJtAw)
3. [Welch Labs](https://www.youtube.com/channel/UConVfxXodg78Tzh5nNu85Ew)
4. [Artem Kirsanov](https://www.youtube.com/@ArtemKirsanov)


I also happened to finally secure a new full time job at my current company [Mindbridge](https://www.mindbridge.ai/) where we run a bunch of unsupervised ML algorithms on financial data ... it's good to see that my effort studying this degree actually paid off :)
