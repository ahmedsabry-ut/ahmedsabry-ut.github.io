I'm writing this blog post shortly after I completed my last assignment in my last ever course, Principles of Data Science. Its funny how I'm taking a "principles" class at the end of my degree after I completed all the hard courses. To be honest, if it were up to me, I wouldn't have taken this class, I would've taken either the Optimization class or the Advances in Deep Learning class, I only took it because it was a graduation requirement.

In hindsight, I'm glad I took this class. I can appreciate having a chill semester after 2 years and a bit of juggling school, life, and work. Especially considering the fact that I had just been laid off and was preparing for married life, I kind of wanted to take it easy. I feel like I just about crossed the finish line.

![We did it meme](/assets/images/memes/we-did-it.jpeg)

The course itself wasn't something useless either, quite the contrary, I appreciate courses such as this one that actually teach you the essence of the degree and Dr. Roger Peng is also too good at his job. I was mostly familiar with R and the core concepts, but I got to learn more data-sciency stuff. The course put an emphasis on both technical and non-technical aspects, which is important, because data scientists are more responsible for telling a story about the data than just knowing how to make models.

Things like TidyModels/Workflows were super interesting because its more of proof-of-concept methodology thing that data scientists would use to test out different models/transforms/data, not something you'd typically find in a production environment.
Propensity scores/matching were also something that I hadn't learned of before that I now appreciate. The assignments were pretty chill too. Just break them down over the course of a couple of days and they'll finish themselves.

I think it's a good decision that they updated the program requirements to include this course as a foundational course. The new program of work now designates this class as a "Gateway Course", so you can't graduate from this program without it now.

I was generally focusing a bit more on my new job this semester anyways, so this was a sweet way to end of the degree!
