This semester was my favourite semester. I took only 1 course in this semester mainly because I had been so burnt out from studying for the Machine Learning class, but this was also a class that I really wanted to focus on. Deep Learning is all the hype nowadays, and rightfully so. The applications you can do with it are so cool and it essentially all boils do to ... "if you have a black box, and you have the inputs and outputs, you can essentially mimic the black box".

![Deep Learning Meme](/assets/images/memes/dl.jpeg)

I got to learn everything about deep learning from the ground up. Started with neurons, activation functions, MLPs, then built our way towards CNNs, transformers, etc. The course content itself was extremely digestible. Dr. Philipp Krähenbühl did a great job of breaking down the content and presenting it in a great manner. The assignments themselves were challenging and fun. Towards the end of the course, I had to make use of Google Colab to actually train the networks because it just took forever to train on my Mac.

A quick note on the professor though, Dr. X is a great lecturer and he offers the course for free on [his website](https://ut.philkr.net/deeplearning/) too. I'd highly recommend going through his lectures if you want to get a feel for the course before enrolling or just for curiosity.

It was also after I finished this class that I tried to do something with this knowledge at work. I worked in a context where we had this "engine" that did all the heavy lifting.
